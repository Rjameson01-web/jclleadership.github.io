When JCL Is Brought In

Most teams know what is happening. They do not correct it. Not because they lack capability — because correcting it requires decisions they avoid. The cycle continues.

The signals

  • Pricing becomes reactive — exceptions multiply, margins drift
  • Forecasting loses credibility — optimism replaces pipeline fact
  • Ownership blurs across functions — handoffs break, escalations rise
  • Execution becomes inconsistent — performance varies by person, not system
  • Decisions stall without clear authority — teams wait rather than act

The root cause

The underlying cause is always the same: ambiguity in mandate, ownership, and decision rights.

When structure is unclear, the commercial engine cannot absorb volatility. Effort increases. Predictability decreases. Drift becomes the operating norm.

This is not a sales problem. It is a control problem.
